\section{Executive Decision}
\textbf{Editorial recommendation: Major Revision.}

The manuscript has a credible and TCSVT-relevant core: it frames CT-to-X-ray transfer as a cost-preserving visual inference problem, uses a same-patient paired cohort, reports a fixed transfer gate, includes matched mechanism controls, and is unusually cautious about clinical readiness. The paper is substantially stronger than a simple pilot because it now reports a 510-patient paired BIMCV audit and separates the validated JDCNet channel from failed comparator channels.

However, it is not yet submission-ready for TCSVT. The main blockers are: (i) venue compliance risks, especially the current \texttt{IEEEtran} option and a 211-word abstract against the TCSVT 150--200 word guidance; (ii) insufficient positioning against very recent privileged-modality and cross-modal medical-transfer work; (iii) incomplete handling of the authors' own March 2026 arXiv pilot/preprint and CrossRef similarity risk; (iv) limited external validity because the positive result remains bounded to a single public paired COVID/non-COVID cohort; and (v) statistical fragility from only 15 fold/seed cells per configuration, requiring stronger patient-level uncertainty reporting and clearer pre-specification evidence.

\section{EIC Review}
\subsection*{Summary}
The manuscript proposes JDCNet, a confidence-gated privileged-modality distillation framework in which CT supervision is used only during training and the deployed model remains an X-ray-only ResNet-18. Two JDCNet configurations pass a pre-specified transfer gate on a 510-patient same-patient BIMCV cohort, while several comparator mechanisms do not. The manuscript positions the contribution as cost-preserving visual inference rather than clinical diagnosis.

\subsection*{Strengths}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item The TCSVT positioning is plausible: the paper addresses image/video learning and understanding under a fixed deployment budget.
    \item The writing is careful about clinical limitations and avoids overclaiming deployment readiness.
    \item The same-patient paired design, fixed transfer gate, and comparator audit make the result more credible than a small-cohort pilot.
    \item The deployment-cost table directly supports the systems claim that the selected JDCNet student adds no inference-time cost relative to the matched supervised X-ray baseline.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Major Weaknesses}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item The novelty statement is still not strong enough for TCSVT. The paper must explain why confidence-gated hard/soft auxiliary targets are not just a thresholded form of reliability-aware distillation or privileged-modality learning.
    \item Venue compliance needs correction before submission: class option, abstract length, prior preprint disclosure, and supplementary packaging.
    \item The paper depends heavily on one public paired COVID cohort. TCSVT reviewers may ask whether the conclusion generalizes to non-COVID thoracic labels, other hospitals, or stronger X-ray backbones.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{EIC Recommendation}
Major Revision. The work is potentially publishable if the authors fix venue compliance, sharpen novelty, and either add external evidence or make the bounded-evidence status even more explicit and quantitatively defensible.

\section{Reviewer 1: Methodology and Statistics}
\subsection*{Strengths}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Patient-level 5-fold cross-validation and seed-paired differences are appropriate for the paired-data question.
    \item The fixed gate, win counts, and bootstrap confidence intervals are useful and more transparent than only reporting point estimates.
    \item The paper reports negative comparator results, which improves scientific credibility.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Major Concerns}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Fold/seed cells are not independent.} The primary evidence uses 15 fold/seed cells per configuration. These cells reuse the same patients and should not be treated as independent experimental units. The manuscript mentions a patient-level paired bootstrap but does not expose the full patient-level results in the main paper or appendix.
    \item \textbf{Hyperparameter search remains a risk.} The 16-cell JDCNet grid and several comparator grids are described, but the exact pre-specification artifact is not reader-verifiable from the paper. A reviewer can still argue that the passing cells are selected from a small grid on the same cohort.
    \item \textbf{External validity is weak.} A single 510-patient public paired cohort with 113 positives cannot establish generality under hospital, scanner, prevalence, and label-definition shift.
    \item \textbf{Calibration evidence is promising but incomplete.} The paper discusses retained/rejected teacher accuracy and ECE, but it should show class-stratified reliability, per-fold variability, and whether the gate threshold was selected without peeking at test outcomes.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Required Methodological Revisions}
Add a compact patient-level uncertainty table for the two passing JDCNet cells, the CT teacher upper bound, and the strongest failed comparators. Include patient-bootstrap CIs, fold/seed CIs, win counts, and a note that the patient bootstrap is the more conservative inference layer. If external paired data cannot be added, state explicitly that the result is a same-cohort mechanism validation, not a generalization result.

\section{Reviewer 2: Domain, Literature, and Clinical Imaging}
\subsection*{Strengths}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item The manuscript correctly distinguishes training-only privileged supervision from inference-time multimodal fusion.
    \item It avoids clinical readiness claims and includes ethics/data caveats for public de-identified data.
    \item It evaluates several plausible transfer channels and reports that many fail, which is useful for the medical-imaging KD literature.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Major Concerns}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Recent related work is incomplete.} The paper should position itself against recent privileged-modality and cross-modal medical-imaging transfer work, including DANTE (AAAI 2026), K-MaT (arXiv 2026), and the MICCAI 2025 CXR--CTPA cross-modal KD work. These papers are not identical, but they occupy the same reviewer mental space.
    \item \textbf{The authors' own arXiv pilot must be handled transparently.} The March 2026 pilot already uses the JDCNet name and CT-to-X-ray distillation framing. The current paper appears to be an expanded and more positive follow-up. TCSVT reviewers need a clear paragraph explaining the difference.
    \item \textbf{COVID/non-COVID binary classification limits domain significance.} The paper should justify why this task is an acceptable systems proxy for broader CT-to-X-ray privileged supervision, while not implying clinical diagnostic utility.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Required Domain Revisions}
Add a short ``Relation to Recent and Prior CT/X-ray Privileged-Transfer Work'' subsection or paragraph. It should explicitly state which elements are shared with prior work and which are distinctive here: same-patient paired X-ray deployment, no inference-time CT, confidence-gated label-like auxiliary targets, fixed-cost inference, failed-transfer reporting, and patient-level gate testing.

\section{Reviewer 3: Visual Systems and Deployment}
\subsection*{Strengths}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item The deployment boundary is well defined: the teacher, mask, and auxiliary loss are removed at inference.
    \item Reporting CPU, GPU, and edge latency is a strong TCSVT-facing addition.
    \item The module-augmented pilot is properly separated from the selected cost-preserving student.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Major Concerns}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{The systems benchmark is too thin for a transactions paper.} Median latency is helpful, but the table lacks variability, measurement environment, memory, energy/power where relevant, batch-size sensitivity, and preprocessing cost details.
    \item \textbf{Template compliance may alter the systems story.} If \verb|compsoc| changes to standard IEEE journal mode, page count and figure/table placement may change. This needs a rebuild before submission.
    \item \textbf{Reproducibility statement should be reader-facing.} The main paper currently says the pipeline includes ``data manifest construction'' and ``teacher pre-training scripts.'' Replace implementation-file language with a clean reproducibility statement: code, split definitions, training configuration, and aggregation utilities are available in a public repository or supplementary material.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Required Systems Revisions}
Update the deployment table or caption with measurement details: hardware, software stack, input size, precision, batch size, warm-up, number of trials, median plus interquartile range or mean plus standard deviation, and whether preprocessing is included. If memory or edge power was measured, report it; if not, state it as a limitation.

\section{Devil's Advocate Review}
\subsection*{Strongest Counter-Argument}
The central positive claim may be an artifact of same-cohort tuning rather than evidence of a robust transfer mechanism. The method searches a small family of confidence thresholds, target types, teacher views, and auxiliary weights on a single paired cohort with only 113 positive patients. Two cells pass a fixed gate, but most cells do not, and the measured gain is only about 0.033--0.035 balanced accuracy. The paper argues that the grid and gate were pre-specified from a 226-patient pilot, but the manuscript itself does not provide a durable, reader-verifiable pre-registration record. Moreover, because the CT teacher and X-ray student are trained and evaluated on the same cohort structure, the confidence gate might select cohort-specific easy cases rather than transferable disease signal. Without external paired validation, the most conservative interpretation is that JDCNet is a plausible same-cohort mechanism, not a validated general CT-to-X-ray transfer method.

\subsection*{Critical or Major Challenges}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \major: The current paper could be seen as an expanded version of the authors' March 2026 arXiv pilot. Failure to disclose and differentiate it would create a serious editorial risk.
    \item \major: If the paper is submitted with \verb|compsoc| formatting, the final page count and style may not match TCSVT expectations.
    \item \major: The confidence gate may be selecting high-confidence/easy cohort-specific examples rather than transferring CT-specific disease information. The retained-versus-rejected analysis helps, but should be made more rigorous and class-stratified.
    \item \major: The baseline set lacks some recent privileged-modality and cross-modal transfer references. Even if the authors cannot run all these methods, they must explain why the selected comparators are sufficient.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Editorial Synthesis}
The reviewers agree that the manuscript has a meaningful contribution if its claim is framed narrowly: confidence-gated auxiliary targets can transfer part of a CT teacher's same-cohort head-room into an X-ray-only student without changing the deployed inference graph. The reviewers also agree that the current version is not yet TCSVT-ready because several risks are editorial rather than merely stylistic: venue compliance, prior-work transparency, statistical dependence, and external validity.

The highest-priority revision should not be a broad rewrite. Instead, make targeted changes that close likely desk-review and peer-review objections:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item correct TCSVT formatting and abstract compliance;
    \item explicitly handle the authors' own March 2026 arXiv pilot and CrossRef similarity risk;
    \item add recent privileged-modality/cross-modal medical-transfer positioning;
    \item expose conservative patient-level uncertainty and gate-reliability evidence;
    \item improve deployment benchmark reporting; and
    \item fix algorithm/figure numbering and reproducibility phrasing.
\end{enumerate}
