\section*{Purpose of This Integrated Report}
This document consolidates two prior reviewer-style assessments into one
non-redundant revision roadmap. Repeated observations have been merged rather
than restated. The resulting guidance treats the manuscript as a transparent
negative-result and evidence-bounded evaluation study, while identifying the
changes needed to make that framing scientifically coherent and venue-aware.

\section*{Executive Synthesis}
The two assessments converge on several central points. First, the manuscript's
strongest contribution is not the named architecture, but its unusually careful
patient-level evaluation protocol, reproducibility package, and transparent
reporting of failed or unstable cross-modal transfer. Second, the empirical
record does not validate JDCNet as an architecture: the module-augmented variant
is weaker than the simplest logit-transfer control and, in several stress tests,
cross-modal knowledge distillation is effectively tied with or worse than
supervised X-ray training. Third, the primary paired cohort is too small and too
imbalanced to support specificity-sensitive positive claims. Fourth, the larger
BIMCV follow-up strengthens the paper as a falsification audit but does not turn
the method into a validated architecture.

The main revision target should therefore be to remove any residual impression
that the paper proves a new CT-to-X-ray distillation method, and instead present
it as a rigorous evidence-boundary study: CT teachers are feasible, but
cross-modal KD remains unstable and unvalidated at the tested cohort scales.
For TCSVT, this is a high-risk positioning because the paper lacks a clear
circuits/systems/video contribution. If the authors keep TCSVT as the target,
the manuscript must explicitly articulate a visual-systems contribution such as
reproducible evaluation of deployment-only visual inference under privileged
training modalities. If that cannot be done convincingly, a medical-imaging,
biomedical-informatics, or negative-results venue is more appropriate.

\section{Consolidated Strengths to Preserve}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Methodological honesty.} The manuscript clearly distinguishes
        directional trends from statistically supported results and avoids
        claiming clinical readiness.
  \item \textbf{Same-case patient-level evaluation.} The repeated Monte Carlo
        protocol compares all methods on identical held-out patients and makes
        the instability of tiny paired cohorts visible.
  \item \textbf{Negative-result transparency.} The paper reports generic KD
        failures, module-stack degradation, corrected-protocol results, and the
        BIMCV Path-C re-split rather than hiding unfavorable evidence.
  \item \textbf{Reproducibility.} The deterministic settings, CLAIM-style
        reporting, split audits, latency measurements, power analysis, and
        external reproducibility package are valuable and should remain central.
  \item \textbf{Cohort-scale awareness.} The manuscript acknowledges that the
        existing paired data are below the scale needed for definitive method
        ranking; this should be treated as a feature of the paper's scientific
        contribution rather than as a side note.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Priority Revision Actions}

\subsection{Reposition the paper around evidence-bounded evaluation, not JDCNet}
The title, abstract, introduction, and Figure~1 should no longer lead with
JDCNet as a validated architecture. The evidence shows that the named module
stack is not the best-performing configuration. Retitle the paper around the
protocol and the negative-result finding, for example:
\begin{verbatim}
Evidence-Bounded Evaluation of Cross-Modal CT-to-X-ray
Knowledge Distillation under Paired-Cohort Constraints
\end{verbatim}
In the introduction, state the core claim as follows: CT can be used to train a
teacher, but the available evidence does not validate a reliable CT-to-X-ray KD
advantage over supervised X-ray learning. Present DPE, MHRA, and DFPN only as
stress-tested optional modules that were falsified, not as the paper's main
contribution.

\subsection{Resolve the venue-fit risk explicitly}
The paper currently sits between two interpretations. One reviewer view is that
its visual transfer, deployment-only inference, and reproducibility focus can be
made relevant to TCSVT; the other is that it remains a static medical-imaging
pilot without circuits, systems, compression, hardware, or video contribution.
The revision must choose one route:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{If keeping TCSVT:} add a concise motivation that frames the
        work as a visual-systems evaluation problem: privileged training
        modalities are unavailable at deployment, so the systems question is
        whether training-only cross-modal information improves lightweight
        single-modality inference under strict audit controls. Avoid clinical
        efficacy claims.
  \item \textbf{If changing venue:} redirect the manuscript toward IEEE TMI,
        Medical Image Analysis, IEEE JBHI, Pattern Recognition, npj Digital
        Medicine, or a negative-results/methodology venue, where a transparent
        falsification study is more naturally aligned.
\end{itemize}
Do not present both routes simultaneously in the manuscript; choose the final
venue narrative before submission.

\subsection{Calibrate all claims to the final evidence}
Revise every claim-status statement, abstract sentence, conclusion sentence,
and figure caption to follow this hierarchy:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item CT teacher training is feasible and produces a nontrivial privileged
        signal.
  \item Plain cross-modal logit KD is the least unstable cross-modal option in
        the small primary cohort, but it is not statistically superior to the
        X-ray-only baseline.
  \item The BIMCV Path-C balanced-validation re-split reduces the KD advantage
        to an effectively null seed-paired difference.
  \item The module-augmented JDCNet stack is not validated and should not be
        described as an architectural improvement.
\end{enumerate}
Remove language that treats one-sided or post-hoc signals as confirmatory. In
particular, do not frame the earlier BIMCV one-sided borderline result as the
paper's strongest positive evidence after the Path-C re-split.

\subsection{Make cohort-size limitations mathematically unavoidable}
The primary paired cohort has too few negative patients to support stable
specificity, balanced accuracy, MCC, calibration, or threshold conclusions. This
must be stated once clearly in the main text and then handled consistently:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item define median and interquartile range as the primary summary for the
        small-cohort Monte Carlo protocol;
  \item move mean$\pm$SD tables to the appendix or explicitly footnote that the
        SD mostly reflects a one-negative validation draw rather than robust
        patient-level uncertainty;
  \item report paired Wilcoxon tests with the tested family and the relevant
        multiple-comparison threshold;
  \item include the power-analysis implication in the main discussion: a much
        larger same-patient paired cohort is required before specificity-aware
        ranking is answerable.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Simplify and demote the architecture section}
The method section should avoid presenting standard operators as new modules.
Rename or describe DPE, MHRA, and DFPN using standard architectural language,
and remove any implication that new named blocks are validated contributions.
Compress the architecture description and use it to explain what was tested, not
to oversell novelty. A suitable structure is:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item problem formulation and privileged-modality training setup;
  \item student-only, late-fusion, same-modality KD, and cross-modal KD controls;
  \item optional module stress tests;
  \item loss functions and evaluation protocol.
\end{enumerate}
If a TCSVT submission is retained, add only engineering details that are
scientifically relevant: parameter counts, latency measurement protocol,
complexity trade-offs, and why added complexity is not justified by the data.

\subsection{Foreground dataset caveats and shortcut-learning evidence}
The COVID-19 Image Data Collection caveat and the cross-source collapse are not
minor limitations; they directly affect interpretation of the training signal.
Move this discussion closer to the primary results. State that the comparison
matrix may reflect acquisition or source artifacts rather than disease-specific
semantics. Also address whether CT slice selection, X-ray preprocessing, or
source-specific metadata could introduce shortcut correlations.

\subsection{Consolidate experimental presentation}
The current evidence trail is valuable but too diffuse. Merge repeated tables
and figures so that each artifact answers one question:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item keep one main table for the primary Monte Carlo comparison, using the
        chosen primary statistics;
  \item summarize the BIMCV Executions A--F in one appendix table plus a short
        body paragraph;
  \item replace degenerate fixed-split heatmaps with text when all values are
        identical or uninformative;
  \item combine negative module-ablation figures into a compact multi-panel
        summary;
  \item shorten long captions, especially for the BIMCV stress-test table, by
        moving interpretation into body text;
  \item verify the latency row in which adding DPE appears faster than the
        student-only model, and document warm-up, batch size, device, and number
        of repeats.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Strengthen related work without expanding the main narrative}
Add a targeted paragraph or appendix subsection that positions the work against:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item learning using privileged information;
  \item modality hallucination and cross-modal distillation;
  \item RGB-D, audio-visual, and other cross-modal teacher-student studies;
  \item recent KD methods beyond 2022, where relevant;
  \item recent biomedical vision-language or chest-imaging representation
        models, including but not limited to BiomedCLIP-style baselines.
\end{itemize}
The goal is not to claim superiority over this literature, but to clarify why
the present study focuses on same-patient paired CT/CXR evidence boundaries.

\subsection{Polish notation, style, and compliance details}
Perform a final consistency pass covering:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item one notation convention for softmax, sigmoid, temperature, transpose,
        and KD weights;
  \item consistent terminology for cross-modal KD, same-modality KD, plain
        logit KD, and attention transfer;
  \item fewer repetitions of ``falsification-oriented'' and ``evidence-boundary'';
  \item a shorter abstract of roughly IEEE-journal length;
  \item concise figure captions;
  \item DOI, venue capitalization, and BibTeX consistency;
  \item patient-identifier, dataset-license, conflict-of-interest, and
        commercial-affiliation checks.
\end{itemize}

\section{Concrete Drop-In Text Guidance}

\subsection{Suggested abstract framing}
\begin{quote}
We study whether chest CT can provide training-only privileged supervision for
an X-ray-only deployed classifier under same-patient paired-cohort constraints.
Across student-only learning, late fusion, same-modality KD, cross-modal logit
KD, attention transfer, feature hints, published KD baselines, and optional
module-augmented variants, the strongest small-cohort trends do not reach
paired statistical significance. A larger BIMCV stress test and a balanced
validation re-split further show that the CT teacher is feasible but that
cross-modal KD remains effectively tied with supervised X-ray training. The
results therefore do not validate JDCNet as an architecture; instead, they
provide an evidence-bounded audit protocol and a negative-result benchmark for
future paired CT/CXR studies.
\end{quote}

\subsection{Suggested conclusion framing}
\begin{quote}
The evidence supports a limited conclusion: CT can act as a training-only
teacher, but the tested CT-to-X-ray distillation strategies do not yield a
validated advantage over supervised X-ray learning at the available paired
cohort scales. The named module stack is best interpreted as a falsified
complexity increment rather than as a successful architectural contribution.
Future progress requires a substantially larger same-patient paired cohort or a
new geometric/representation anchor that can be evaluated under the same
patient-level protocol.
\end{quote}

\section{Final Consolidated Recommendation}
The manuscript should proceed as a major narrative and presentation revision,
not as another attempt to extract a positive architectural claim from the same
results. The most defensible submission version is a transparent, reproducible,
evidence-bounded negative-result study. If submitted to TCSVT, the authors must
make the visual-systems relevance explicit and accept that the venue fit is
risky. If submitted to a medical-imaging or negative-results venue, the current
evidence trail becomes a strength rather than a mismatch.
