\section{Revised Abstract (Template)}

\begin{abstract}
Chest X-ray and computed tomography (CT) provide complementary views of thoracic disease, yet most existing computer-aided diagnosis pipelines are trained on a single imaging modality. This limits the reuse of labeled data and under-utilizes paired examinations that frequently occur in clinical workflows. In this paper, we study cross-modality knowledge distillation for chest image classification and ask whether a teacher model trained on CT can provide useful supervisory signals for a student model operating on X-ray images. We propose JDCNet, a teacher--student framework that combines a modality-specific teacher branch, a lightweight student branch, and a distillation objective that aligns class-level predictions across modalities. The architecture includes a Deep Probe Expedition (DPE) module to emphasize discriminative regions before distillation, a Multi-Head Retain Area (MHRA) module to aggregate class-relevant teacher tokens, and a Distillate Feature Pyramid Network (DFPN) on the student side to absorb transferred knowledge. We evaluate JDCNet on a curated cohort of paired chest X-ray and CT images derived from a public COVID-19 collection, using patient-level splits and repeated runs for robust estimation. On the paired X-ray target task, cross-modality distillation improves the student performance over student-only training and same-modality distillation in terms of accuracy, macro-F1, and balanced accuracy under the present protocol. All manifests, training scripts, and result summaries are released as an executable pipeline to facilitate reproduction and future extensions on larger cohorts.
\end{abstract}

\section{Revised Contributions}

In Section~1.2, the contributions can be rewritten as:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Problem formulation.} We formalize cross-modality thoracic image classification as a teacher--student knowledge distillation problem, where a CT-based teacher transfers class-discriminative information to an X-ray-based student under realistic patient-level pairing constraints.
  \item \textbf{Architecture.} We introduce JDCNet, a distillation framework that integrates a region-selection module (DPE), a token-aggregation module (MHRA), and a student-side feature pyramid (DFPN) to preserve modality-specific cues while exposing transferable supervisory signals.
  \item \textbf{Executable evaluation pipeline.} We implement a fully executable experiment pipeline on a public COVID-19 chest imaging collection, including manifest generation, patient-level splitting, repeated-run evaluation, and automatic export of tables and figures, and show that cross-modality distillation improves the X-ray target performance over strong student-only and same-modality baselines under the current cohort.
\end{itemize}
