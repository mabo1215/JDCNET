\section*{Review Report for IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (TCSVT)}

\subsection*{Overall Recommendation}
\textbf{Recommendation: Major Revision before submission to TCSVT.}

The paper proposes JDCNet, a confidence-gated cross-modal distillation framework that uses CT teacher predictions as training-only auxiliary supervision for an X-ray-only student classifier. The central idea is potentially interesting for efficient visual inference because the deployment path uses only X-ray and therefore does not increase inference-time modality requirements. The manuscript also makes a serious effort to report patient-level cross-validation, matched baselines, mechanism-level ablations, bootstrap confidence intervals, and deployment-time efficiency.

However, in its current form, the paper is not yet ready for IEEE TCSVT submission. The main concerns are: 
(1) the manuscript is far beyond the TCSVT regular-paper length limit if the appendix is included in the submitted PDF; 
(2) the TCSVT circuits/systems/video-technology motivation is still weaker than the medical-imaging narrative; 
(3) there are severe internal inconsistencies in dataset descriptions, backbone definitions, parameter counts, and equation references; 
(4) the claimed novelty and ``first'' statement require stronger positioning against cross-modal distillation, privileged information learning, modality hallucination, and medical foundation-model baselines; 
(5) the empirical effect size is modest and the validation remains single-cohort, so the claims should be carefully bounded.

\subsection*{Summary of Strengths}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Clear deployment constraint.} The paper studies a practically meaningful setting: CT is available only during training, while inference must remain X-ray-only.
    
    \item \textbf{Cost-aware design.} The proposed confidence-gated auxiliary supervision is attractive because the teacher branch is removed at deployment, preserving the inference-time structure of the student model.
    
    \item \textbf{Better-than-usual evaluation discipline.} The use of patient-level cross-validation, matched supervised baselines, paired deltas, bootstrap confidence intervals, and explicit gate criteria is stronger than many small-cohort medical-imaging submissions.
    
    \item \textbf{Useful negative evidence.} The manuscript reports that several alternative mechanisms fail the same gate. This is scientifically useful and can support the claim that the transfer channel, not merely the teacher representation or backbone capacity, is critical.
    
    \item \textbf{Reproducibility orientation.} The paper repeatedly refers to manifests, scripts, decision reports, and a public artifact. This is a strong point for TCSVT if made immutable and easy to verify.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Major Concerns and Required Revisions}

\subsubsection*{M1. The manuscript is not yet formatted as a TCSVT-ready regular paper}

\textbf{Concern.}
The submitted PDF contains the full appendix and reaches approximately 30 pages. This is not suitable as a TCSVT main manuscript. The abstract is also longer than the TCSVT recommended 150--200 word range.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Compress the main manuscript to a self-contained TCSVT paper and move extensive sweep tables, stress tests, and historical experiments to supplementary material.

\textbf{Suggested main-paper structure.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Introduction: 1.5--2 pages.
    \item Related Work: 1.5--2 pages.
    \item Method: 2 pages.
    \item Experimental Protocol: 1.5 pages.
    \item Main Results: 3--4 pages.
    \item Deployment Efficiency: 1 page.
    \item Limitations and Conclusion: 1 page.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Suggested abstract replacement.}
\begin{quote}
\small
We present JDCNet, a confidence-gated cross-modal distillation framework for training an X-ray classifier with CT supervision while preserving an X-ray-only deployment path. A CT teacher is used only during training; at inference, the deployed student receives only X-ray input and has the same parameter, MAC, and latency budget as the supervised X-ray baseline. JDCNet applies an auxiliary teacher target only when the CT teacher confidence exceeds a threshold, using either a hard argmax target or a temperature-scaled soft-KL target. On 510 same-patient paired BIMCV cases under patient-level 5-fold cross-validation, two pre-specified configurations pass a statistical transfer gate against the matched supervised baseline: a 3-slice CT teacher with soft-KL supervision and a mid-slice CT teacher with hard supervision. Under the same protocol, gated logit distillation, contrastive feature alignment, attention transfer, feature hints, BiomedCLIP fine-tuning, and a module-augmented variant do not pass the gate. These results suggest that confidence-gated auxiliary targets are more transferable than uniformly softened logits for this CT-to-X-ray setting, although external paired-cohort validation remains necessary.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M2. TCSVT scope needs a stronger circuits/systems framing}

\textbf{Concern.}
The manuscript is currently dominated by medical-imaging and COVID-19 classification framing. TCSVT can accept image/video learning papers, but the paper must clearly contribute to video/image systems, efficient deployment, or circuits-and-systems-oriented visual processing. The present systems argument is present but not yet dominant enough.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Reframe the paper as an efficient visual inference system under a training-only privileged-modality constraint. Move clinical language into the background and limitations. Emphasize:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item single-modality deployment under auxiliary-modality training;
    \item deployment cost invariance;
    \item MACs, parameters, latency, memory, and energy;
    \item edge/embedded inference relevance;
    \item modality-availability constraints as a systems problem.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Suggested title revision.}
\begin{quote}
\small
\textit{JDCNet: Confidence-Gated Privileged-Modality Distillation for Cost-Preserving X-ray Inference}
\end{quote}

\textbf{Suggested introduction sentence.}
\begin{quote}
\small
This work studies a systems-level visual inference problem: how to exploit an expensive auxiliary modality during training while preserving a fixed-cost, single-modality deployment path at inference.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M3. Backbone and efficiency numbers are internally inconsistent}

\textbf{Concern.}
The main text repeatedly states that the student is a standard ResNet-18, but the deployment-efficiency table reports only approximately $0.094$M parameters for the student. A standard ResNet-18 has roughly 11M parameters, so the reported parameter count is inconsistent with the claimed backbone. The paper also alternates between 128$\times$128 training resolution and 224$\times$224 deployment-efficiency measurement. This undermines the deployment-cost claim, which is central for TCSVT.

\textbf{Required revision.}
The authors must define exactly which student backbone is used in each experiment:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item custom lightweight CNN;
    \item standard torchvision ResNet-18;
    \item modified/narrow ResNet-18;
    \item ImageNet-pretrained ResNet-18;
    \item BiomedCLIP ViT-B/16.
\end{enumerate}
For each backbone, report parameters, MACs, input size, training resolution, testing resolution, and latency under the same measurement protocol.

\subsubsection*{M4. Dataset descriptions are confusing and sometimes contradictory}

\textbf{Concern.}
The manuscript mixes several cohorts: the Cohen COVID-19 Image Data Collection, BIMCV-COVID19+, a 226-patient balanced cohort, a 510-patient BIMCV cohort, a 512-patient stress test, a 228-patient balanced BIMCV subset, MIDRC experiments, and cross-source normal controls. The main experiment is supposed to be the 510-patient BIMCV paired cohort, but Section 4 begins by saying the study is evaluated on the Cohen COVID-19 Image Data Collection. This creates serious confusion about which dataset supports the headline claim.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Separate the datasets into:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Primary validation cohort:} 510 same-patient paired BIMCV cases.
    \item \textbf{Historical feasibility cohort:} 226-patient earlier paired subset.
    \item \textbf{Stress-test / exploratory cohorts:} 512-patient BIMCV stress test, MIDRC pilot, cross-source controls.
\end{enumerate}
Only the primary cohort should be used to support the headline claim.

\textbf{Suggested replacement for the opening of the Experiments section.}
\begin{quote}
\small
The primary evidence in this paper is obtained from the 510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort. The earlier 226-patient cohort and MIDRC/cross-source experiments are retained only as historical feasibility or stress-test analyses and are not used to establish the main claim.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M5. Equation numbering and method references are incorrect}

\textbf{Concern.}
The JDCNet loss is numbered as Eq.~(1), while the comparator logit-KD objective is numbered as Eq.~(2). However, the text later states that the comparator uses Eq.~(1) and that JDCNet is Eq.~(3). This is mathematically confusing and must be fixed.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Use explicit equation labels:
\begin{verbatim}
\label{eq:jdcnet_loss}
\label{eq:logit_kd_loss}
\end{verbatim}
Then refer to them by name:
\begin{verbatim}
Eq.~\eqref{eq:jdcnet_loss}
Eq.~\eqref{eq:logit_kd_loss}
\end{verbatim}

\textbf{Suggested corrected wording.}
\begin{quote}
\small
The legacy comparator family optimizes the logit-KD objective in Eq.~\eqref{eq:logit_kd_loss}, whereas JDCNet optimizes the confidence-gated auxiliary objective in Eq.~\eqref{eq:jdcnet_loss}.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M6. The novelty claim is too strong without additional evidence}

\textbf{Concern.}
The statement ``the first cross-modal CT-to-X-ray transfer method validated under a pre-registered statistical gate on 100+ same-patient paired patients with public data'' is narrow and possibly defensible, but it reads as overly strong. Reviewers may challenge it unless the authors provide a systematic literature table covering cross-modal medical distillation, privileged information learning, modality hallucination, CT-to-CXR synthesis/translation, and multimodal thoracic learning.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Either provide a detailed novelty comparison table or soften the claim.

\textbf{Suggested softened wording.}
\begin{quote}
\small
To the best of our knowledge, this is among the first studies to evaluate training-only CT-to-X-ray distillation under a pre-specified patient-level statistical gate on a public same-patient paired cohort.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M7. The empirical gain is small and must be carefully bounded}

\textbf{Concern.}
The two passing JDCNet configurations improve balanced accuracy by only about $+0.033$ to $+0.035$. Although the bootstrap confidence intervals are positive, the effect is modest and still within a single-cohort evaluation setting. The current conclusion sometimes sounds stronger than the evidence supports.

\textbf{Required revision.}
The conclusion should explicitly state that the evidence validates the \emph{transfer-channel mechanism under this cohort and protocol}, not clinical utility or general deployment readiness.

\textbf{Suggested limitation sentence.}
\begin{quote}
\small
The observed gains should be interpreted as evidence that confidence-gated auxiliary targets are a promising transfer channel under this cohort and protocol, not as evidence of clinical readiness or guaranteed external generalization.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M8. Pre-registration needs stronger proof}

\textbf{Concern.}
The manuscript states that the gate was committed before the extension sweeps, but the paper does not provide an immutable commit hash, timestamped archive, DOI, or exact file snapshot. Since the positive result depends heavily on the pre-registered gate, reviewers will expect verifiable evidence.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Provide:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item repository URL;
    \item exact commit hash;
    \item timestamp;
    \item archived DOI or Code Ocean capsule identifier;
    \item exact filenames for the pre-registration plan;
    \item command used to reproduce Table~28.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Suggested wording.}
\begin{quote}
\small
The transfer gate and sweep matrix were fixed before running Extension-A and Extension-B. The immutable record is available at commit \texttt{<commit-hash>} in \texttt{docs/future\_methods\_plan.md} and archived in \texttt{<DOI/capsule-ID>}.
\end{quote}

\subsubsection*{M9. The experimental narrative is too complex for the main paper}

\textbf{Concern.}
The main paper currently contains historical fixed-split results, 226-patient resampling, 510-patient validation, 512-patient stress tests, MIDRC pilots, BiomedCLIP frozen probes, BiomedCLIP fine-tuning, DRR experiments, and many negative controls. This makes the paper look like an experimental log rather than a polished TCSVT article.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Keep only the following in the main text:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item primary 510-patient JDCNet validation;
    \item CT teacher upper-bound result;
    \item matched comparator audit;
    \item deployment-cost table;
    \item one concise limitations paragraph.
\end{enumerate}
Move all historical and exploratory experiments to supplementary material.

\subsubsection*{M10. The clinical/ethics discussion should be more precise}

\textbf{Concern.}
The paper states that no additional IRB approval is required because the data are public and de-identified. This is plausible, but the statement should be tied to dataset licenses and institutional policy. Also, the paper should avoid implying diagnostic usefulness.

\textbf{Required revision.}
Add a paragraph clarifying:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item no patient-level re-identification was attempted;
    \item no pixel data are redistributed;
    \item the work is a methodological systems study;
    \item the model is not intended for clinical decision-making;
    \item external validation is mandatory before any deployment claim.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Minor Comments}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item The abstract contains too many numbers. Keep only the two passing configurations and move other details to the results section.
    
    \item Use consistent terminology: choose one among ``JDCNet'', ``confidence-gated KD'', and ``confidence-gated cross-modal distillation'', and define all variants once.
    
    \item Avoid all-caps ``FAIL'' in the abstract. Use ``do not pass the gate'' for a more academic tone.
    
    \item Replace ``largest publicly-available same-patient paired thoracic cohort known to us'' with a more cautious statement unless supported by a systematic dataset survey.
    
    \item Clarify whether the 510-patient BIMCV cohort uses one CXR per patient or multiple CXR images per patient. The paper alternates between image-level and patient-level counts.
    
    \item Report confidence-gate active ratios for the two passing JDCNet configurations.
    
    \item Report sensitivity, specificity, macro-F1, ROC-AUC, and PR-AUC for the two passing configurations in one main table.
    
    \item Add calibration results for JDCNet specifically, not only for historical methods.
    
    \item Use ``balanced accuracy'' consistently as ``BA'' after first definition.
    
    \item Replace ``student recovers roughly two-thirds of the CT teacher head-room'' with an explicit formula:
    \[
    \mathrm{Recovery} =
    \frac{\mathrm{BA}_{\mathrm{JDCNet}}-\mathrm{BA}_{\mathrm{Xray}}}
    {\mathrm{BA}_{\mathrm{CT}}-\mathrm{BA}_{\mathrm{Xray}}}.
    \]
    
    \item The deployment-efficiency table should include batch size, device, PyTorch version, CUDA version, precision, and whether CPU latency uses single-thread or multi-thread execution.
    
    \item Figures 2 and 3 are difficult to interpret because historical and primary evidence are visually mixed. Separate historical diagnostics from the main result.
    
    \item The paper should explicitly state whether validation hyperparameters were selected using test folds. If the same 5-fold CV was used for both selection and reporting, nested CV or a validation split is preferred.
    
    \item The comparison with BiomedCLIP should include input preprocessing, image normalization, whether the visual tower was fully fine-tuned, and parameter count.
    
    \item The manuscript should include a clear statement that late fusion is not deployment-realistic because it requires CT at inference.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Suggested Revised Contribution List}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item We formulate CT-to-X-ray distillation as a training-only privileged-modality problem for cost-preserving X-ray deployment.
    
    \item We propose a confidence-gated auxiliary target that transfers CT teacher predictions only when the teacher is sufficiently confident, while keeping the true-label X-ray loss always active.
    
    \item We validate the method on a 510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort using patient-level 5-fold cross-validation, paired deltas, and a pre-specified statistical gate.
    
    \item We show that several alternative transfer channels, including logit KD, gated logit KD, contrastive alignment, attention transfer, feature hints, module-augmented KD, and BiomedCLIP fine-tuning, do not pass the same gate.
    
    \item We report deployment-time cost and show that the validated JDCNet student preserves the same inference-time path as the supervised X-ray baseline.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Suggested Revised Conclusion}

\begin{quote}
\small
This paper studied training-only CT supervision for cost-preserving X-ray deployment. Under a 510-patient same-patient BIMCV protocol with patient-level 5-fold cross-validation and a pre-specified transfer gate, two confidence-gated JDCNet configurations improved balanced accuracy over the matched supervised X-ray baseline while preserving the same X-ray-only inference path. In contrast, uniformly softened logit distillation, feature-space contrastive alignment, attention transfer, feature hints, module-augmented KD, and BiomedCLIP fine-tuning did not pass the same gate. These findings suggest that the transfer channel, rather than teacher availability alone, is the key bottleneck in CT-to-X-ray privileged-modality learning. The result remains bounded to a single public paired cohort and binary COVID/non-COVID classification; independent paired-cohort validation is required before any clinical or production deployment claim.
\end{quote}

\subsection*{Final Assessment}

The paper has a promising idea and a stronger evaluation discipline than many small-cohort medical-imaging papers. However, it must be substantially reorganized before TCSVT submission. The highest-priority fixes are:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item compress the main paper to the TCSVT page limit;
    \item strengthen the visual-systems and deployment-efficiency framing;
    \item fix backbone, parameter-count, input-resolution, and equation-number inconsistencies;
    \item make the primary 510-patient BIMCV result the clear center of the paper;
    \item move historical and exploratory experiments to supplementary material;
    \item soften novelty and clinical implications;
    \item provide immutable reproducibility evidence for the pre-registration and result tables.
\end{enumerate}

If these revisions are completed, the manuscript could become a credible TCSVT submission as an efficient visual inference / privileged-modality distillation paper. Without these revisions, the paper is likely to face either desk rejection for formatting/scope issues or major reviewer criticism for internal inconsistency and over-claiming.
