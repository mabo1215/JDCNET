Cover Letter
Submission to IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (TCSVT)

Title: JDCNet: Confidence-Gated Privileged-Modality Distillation for
       Cost-Preserving X-ray Inference

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "JDCNet: Confidence-Gated
Privileged-Modality Distillation for Cost-Preserving X-ray Inference," for
consideration in IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video
Technology.

This paper studies a practical visual-inference setting in which CT is
available during training but the deployed model must use X-ray alone. We
propose JDCNet, a confidence-gated CT-to-X-ray distillation method. The CT
teacher provides an auxiliary hard or lightly softened target only when its
confidence exceeds a fixed threshold; at test time, the CT branch and the
auxiliary loss are removed. The deployed student is therefore the same
single-modality ResNet-18 X-ray classifier as the supervised baseline.

On a 510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort, using patient-level
5-fold cross-validation and seeds 42--44, two JDCNet settings pass the fixed
transfer gate against the matched supervised X-ray baseline:

- 3-slice CT teacher with soft-KL target: Delta BA = +0.0345,
  95% CI [+0.0112, +0.0571].
- Mid-slice CT teacher with hard target: Delta BA = +0.0329,
  95% CI [+0.0074, +0.0584].

The CT teacher upper-bound rows also show positive head-room on the same
patients. In contrast, plain and gated logit distillation, DRR-guided logit
transfer, contrastive alignment, attention transfer, feature hints,
BiomedCLIP fine-tuning, and the historical module-augmented pilot do not
pass the same gate. We therefore present a bounded claim: confidence-gated
auxiliary targets are the supported transfer channel in this cohort, while
external paired-cohort replication is still required before any deployment
claim.

The systems relevance for TCSVT is that JDCNet uses the richer CT modality
only at training time while preserving the deployed X-ray inference graph.
The manuscript reports parameters, MACs, memory, and CPU/GPU/edge latency to
verify that the selected JDCNet student adds no inference-time cost relative
to the supervised X-ray baseline.

The manuscript is prepared as a 14-page TCSVT submission package. The study
is a secondary analysis of publicly released, de-identified human imaging
data. The original BIMCV release reports institutional research-committee
approval and anonymization/de-identification by the data custodians; we did
not collect new patient data, obtain new consent, attempt re-identification,
or redistribute patient pixels.

The work has not been published previously and is not under consideration
elsewhere. Source code, split definitions, training configurations, and
analysis utilities are released at:

https://github.com/mabo1215/JDCNET

Thank you for your time and consideration.
