Cover Letter
Submission to ACM Transactions on Multimedia Computing, Communications, and
Applications (ACM TOMM)

Title: JDCNet: Confidence-Gated Privileged-Modality Distillation for
       Cost-Preserving X-ray Inference

Submission category: Regular Paper (regular submission, no special issue)

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "JDCNet: Confidence-Gated
Privileged-Modality Distillation for Cost-Preserving X-ray Inference," for
consideration as a Regular Paper in ACM Transactions on Multimedia Computing,
Communications, and Applications.

This paper studies a practical multimedia-systems setting in which a richer
imaging modality (CT) is available during training but the deployed model must
use X-ray alone. We propose JDCNet, a confidence-gated CT-to-X-ray distillation
method. The CT teacher provides an auxiliary hard or lightly softened target
only when its confidence exceeds a fixed threshold; at test time, the CT branch
and the auxiliary loss are removed. The deployed student is therefore the same
single-modality ResNet-18 X-ray classifier as the supervised baseline.

On a 510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort, using patient-level 5-fold
cross-validation and seeds 42--44, two JDCNet settings pass the fixed transfer
gate against the matched supervised X-ray baseline:

- 3-slice CT teacher with soft-KL target: Delta BA = +0.0345,
  95% CI [+0.0112, +0.0571].
- Mid-slice CT teacher with hard target: Delta BA = +0.0329,
  95% CI [+0.0074, +0.0584].

The CT teacher upper-bound rows also show positive head-room on the same
patients. In contrast, plain and gated logit distillation, DRR-guided logit
transfer, contrastive alignment, attention transfer, feature hints, BiomedCLIP
fine-tuning, and a module-augmented pilot do not pass the same gate. We
therefore present a bounded claim: confidence-gated auxiliary targets are the
supported transfer channel in this cohort, while external paired-cohort
replication is still required before any deployment claim.

The relevance to ACM TOMM is that JDCNet treats cross-modal supervision as a
training-time signal-routing problem under a fixed deployment budget: the richer
CT modality shapes only the training objective, while the deployed graph remains
a standard single-modality classifier. The manuscript reports parameters, MACs,
memory, and CPU/GPU/edge latency to verify that the selected JDCNet student adds
no inference-time cost relative to the supervised X-ray baseline, which we
believe is of interest to the multimedia computing and communications community
concerned with efficient, deployment-realistic multimodal systems.

In accordance with the ACM TOMM double-blind review policy, the submitted
manuscript is fully anonymized (author names, affiliations, and acknowledgments
are removed, and code/repository identifiers are withheld and provided to
reviewers only as anonymized supplementary material). Author-identifying
information is supplied separately on the title page for editorial use only.

The study is a secondary analysis of publicly released, de-identified human
imaging data. The original BIMCV release reports institutional
research-committee approval and anonymization/de-identification by the data
custodians; we did not collect new patient data, obtain new consent, attempt
re-identification, or redistribute patient pixels.

The work has not been published previously and is not under consideration
elsewhere. Source code, split definitions, training configurations, and analysis
utilities will be released under an open-source licence upon acceptance.
