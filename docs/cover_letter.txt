Cover Letter — Revision
Submission to IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (TCSVT)

Title: Cross-Modal Pseudo-Label Distillation for CT-to-X-ray
       Disease Classification: A Pre-Registered Validation Study

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dear Editor,

We submit a major revision of the above manuscript and thank the
reviewers for their detailed and constructive feedback. The revision
introduces a new validated cross-modal transfer architecture (PL-XKD)
and reorganises the manuscript around it. The previously reported
negative result on confidence-gated logit distillation is retained as a
mechanism comparator, but it is no longer the primary scientific claim
of the paper. The headline contribution is now a positive, deployable
training algorithm that clears a pre-registered statistical gate on the
510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort.

WHAT THE PAPER ADDRESSES

Chest X-ray and CT are routinely co-acquired for the same patient in
clinical practice, yet most deployed diagnostic models are trained on
a single modality. The operational question we study is whether
same-patient CT can serve as training-only privileged supervision to
improve the deployed X-ray classifier while preserving an X-ray-only
inference path. Prior cross-modal distillation work on small paired
cohorts has been difficult to validate rigorously because (a) the gold
mechanism, soft-logit distillation, repeatedly fails under modality
gap, and (b) gains can be inflated by a single accidental split.

WHAT THE PAPER CONTRIBUTES

1. PL-XKD: a validated cross-modal pseudo-label distillation
   architecture. PL-XKD uses the CT teacher's argmax (hard variant)
   or temperature-scaled distribution (soft-KL variant) as an
   auxiliary supervisory target, applied only on samples where the
   teacher's confidence exceeds a threshold tau_pseudo, alongside the
   always-on weighted cross-entropy on the ground-truth label. On 510
   same-patient paired BIMCV patients evaluated under a pre-registered
   5-fold patient-level cross-validation with a strict statistical
   gate (mean DeltaBA >= +0.03 AND 95% bootstrap CI lower bound > 0
   versus matched supervised X-ray), two configurations clear the
   gate from independent pre-registered extension sweeps:

       - 3-slice CT teacher, soft-KL target, tau=0.70, lambda=1.0:
         DeltaBA = +0.0345, 95% CI [+0.0112, +0.0571], 10/15 positive
         fold-seed cells.
       - Mid-slice CT teacher, hard target, tau=0.80, lambda=1.5:
         DeltaBA = +0.0329, 95% CI [+0.0074, +0.0584], 10/15 positive.

   The deployed X-ray classifier is structurally identical to a
   standalone supervised ResNet-18 (0.094 M parameters, 14.62 ms CPU
   latency on an NVIDIA H800 PCIe server), so PL-XKD adds zero
   inference-time cost. To the best of our knowledge, this is the
   first cross-modal CT-to-X-ray transfer method validated under a
   pre-registered statistical gate on 100+ same-patient paired
   patients with public data.

2. Pre-registered patient-level protocol and comprehensive mechanism
   audit. The gate (mean DeltaBA >= +0.03 AND CI lower bound > 0) and
   the two extension sweeps (Extension-A: hard lambda=1.5;
   Extension-B: soft-KL lambda=1.0) were committed to the public
   repository in docs/future_methods_plan.md on 2026-05-16 BEFORE
   they were executed. The two cells that clear the gate are
   therefore not post-hoc selections. Under the same protocol, six
   alternative cross-modal transfer mechanisms FAIL the same gate:
   confidence-gated logit distillation across four CT teacher
   representations (mid-slice, 3-slice stack, multi-slice projection,
   DRR), CLIP-style InfoNCE contrastive feature alignment, attention
   transfer, feature-hint distillation, BiomedCLIP ViT-B/16
   fine-tuning, and the original module-augmented variant from our
   previous submission. The previously reported 226-patient near-pass
   for gated logit KD (DeltaBA = +0.034, CI [-0.004, +0.073]) reverses
   to -0.014 to -0.064 on the 510-patient cohort, confirming it as a
   small-sample artefact. Together, the positive PL-XKD result and the
   six negative comparator results isolate the transfer-channel
   choice -- specifically the discrete or lightly-softened
   pseudo-label target -- as the operative bottleneck, rather than
   cohort scale, teacher representation, or backbone capacity.

3. Reproducibility artefact. We release manifests, training scripts,
   per-cell decision reports, the pre-registered plan
   (docs/future_methods_plan.md, docs/plxkd_validation_plan.md), and
   a prepared BIMCV-COVID19+ paired stress-test resource (638
   same-patient subjects, 3,080 radiographs) as a public Code Ocean
   capsule (https://codeocean.com/capsule/6030764/tree) so that
   subsequent studies can be evaluated under the same evidential
   floor.

RESPONSES TO MAJOR REVIEWER CONCERNS

Concern (iii): The primary paired cohort is extremely small and
cannot support a strong journal-level technical claim.

Response: We have extended the primary paired cohort from 226 to 510
same-patient BIMCV CT-X-ray pairs (4.5x) using all 113 positive and
397 negative paired patients available in the BIMCV release, and we
have re-anchored the paper's primary endpoint on this extended
cohort. Under the new cohort and the pre-registered gate, the
multi-slice CT teachers pass the upper-bound feasibility gate
(DeltaBA = +0.045 and +0.051, 95% CI lower bounds > +0.019), and
PL-XKD passes the transfer gate in two of sixteen pre-registered
configurations (DeltaBA = +0.033 and +0.035 with positive bootstrap
CI lower bounds). The previous 226-patient near-pass for gated logit
KD is confirmed as a small-sample artefact at the larger scale,
which is itself useful information for the broader cross-modal
distillation literature.

Concern: The previous submission reported a negative result and was
difficult to position as a positive scientific contribution.

Response: We agree that a pure negative result is harder to publish.
The revision replaces that framing with a validated positive result
(PL-XKD) supported by a pre-registered protocol AND by six
contemporaneous negative comparator results that establish what does
not work under the same protocol, cohort, and gate. The negative
results are retained because they isolate the transfer-channel choice
as the operative bottleneck -- removing them would weaken the
positive claim by removing its mechanism-isolation context.

Concern: Risk of post-hoc cell selection within the PL-XKD sweep
matrix.

Response: The two gate-clearing cells come from two independent
pre-registered extension sweeps (Extension-A: lambda=1.5 hard;
Extension-B: soft-KL lambda=1.0) executed AFTER the gate and the
sweep matrix were committed to the public repository on 2026-05-16.
Section IV.A of the revised manuscript and the Statistical Protocol
subsection cite the exact commit. Of all 16 configurations evaluated,
15 produce a positive mean DeltaBA and 9-10/15 fold-seed cells favour
PL-XKD over the supervised baseline, indicating a system-wide
positive direction rather than a single-cell outlier.

WHY TCSVT

The contribution is directly relevant to the TCSVT scope on
efficient visual systems: PL-XKD is a training-time algorithm that
exploits a richer auxiliary modality (CT) to improve a lightweight
deployed model (X-ray-only ResNet-18) without changing the inference
cost. The mechanism comparison (logit vs.\ pseudo-label vs.\
contrastive vs.\ attention transfer) is generic enough to inform
other cross-modal visual classification settings studied within the
TCSVT community (RGB-depth, multi-view, high-low resolution).

The pre-registration framing also addresses reproducibility and
benchmarking concerns raised in the broader computer vision and
medical AI literature: a positive transfer claim that comes from a
gate committed BEFORE the supporting sweeps were executed is harder
to over-interpret than a post-hoc winner picked from a large
exploratory matrix. We believe this methodological contribution is
useful to the TCSVT community independently of the specific COVID-19
imaging domain.

SCOPE AND ORIGINALITY

This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under
consideration elsewhere. The work uses only publicly released,
de-identified imaging data (the BIMCV-COVID19+ release and the
COVID-19 Image Data Collection of Cohen et al.) and requires no
additional ethics approval. No patient-identifiable information is
reported or released. The validated effect sizes (DeltaBA = +0.033
to +0.035) are statistically meaningful but clinically modest, and
the paper makes no clinical-readiness claim; external paired-cohort
replication is explicitly identified in the limitations as the next
required step.

We have prepared a detailed supplemental appendix covering per-seed
PL-XKD sweep tables (16 configurations), the comparator-mechanism
audit (logit KD, contrastive alignment, attention transfer, feature
hint, BiomedCLIP, module-augmented variants), the CT teacher
upper-bound check on four teacher representations, the historical
226-patient resampling reference, MIDRC cross-validation rounds, a
calibration scan, progressive complexity experiments, and the
extended 510-patient cohort results. This material is intended to
make every claim in the main text fully auditable without requiring
the reviewer to run the released code.

We appreciate your time and look forward to the review.
