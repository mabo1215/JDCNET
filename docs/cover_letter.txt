Cover Letter — Revision
Submission to IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (TCSVT)

Title: JDCNet: Cross-Modal CT-to-X-ray Knowledge Distillation with
       Evidence-Bounded Evaluation on Patient-Level Paired Cohorts

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dear Editor,

We submit a revised version of the above manuscript and thank the reviewers
for their detailed and constructive feedback. The revision addresses all
major concerns raised in the review; the most significant change is a
$4.5\times$ scale extension of the primary same-patient paired cohort, which
directly resolves reviewer concern (iii) regarding cohort size.

WHAT THE PAPER ADDRESSES

Chest X-ray and CT are routinely co-acquired for the same patient in
clinical practice, yet most deployed diagnostic models are trained and
evaluated on a single modality. The question we study is operationally
direct: if same-patient CT and X-ray are both available during training
but only X-ray is available at inference, can CT serve as training-only
privileged supervision to improve the deployed X-ray classifier?

We formulate this as a teacher-student knowledge distillation problem
(CT teacher, X-ray student) and evaluate it on a patient-level paired
chest cohort. The central challenge is not architectural -- it is
evidential: the paired cohort is small, class-imbalanced, and easy to
over-read under a single fixed validation split.

WHAT THE PAPER CONTRIBUTES

1. Falsification-oriented evaluation protocol. We introduce same-case
   patient-level Monte Carlo resampling (10 resamples, each holding out
   the identical set of patients for all compared methods) as the
   minimum evidential bar for tiny-cohort cross-modal distillation.
   We show that this bar is sufficient to reject a fixed-split ranking
   produced under the same data.

2. Definitive negative result at 4.5x cohort scale. We evaluate CT
   teacher representations (mid-slice, 3-slice stack, multi-slice
   projection, DRR), a fine-tuned foundation-model student
   (BiomedCLIP ViT-B/16), a full temperature-threshold calibration
   scan, and an extended 510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort
   that uses all 113 positive and 397 negative paired patients in the
   release (4.5x the previous balanced cohort). At the extended scale,
   multi-slice CT teachers (mid-slice and 3-slice stack) now pass the
   pre-specified teacher upper-bound gate with substantial effect sizes
   (mean DeltaBA = +0.045 and +0.051, 95% bootstrap CI lower bounds
   > +0.019), confirming that CT carries patient-level disease
   information. However, confidence-gated logit KD fails the gate
   across all four teacher representations; DRR-guided gated KD shows
   a catastrophic specificity collapse (mean DeltaBA = -0.064, 95% CI
   [-0.095, -0.034]). The 226-patient calibration "near-pass"
   (DeltaBA = +0.034, CI [-0.004, +0.073]) is confirmed as a
   small-sample artifact. The bottleneck is the cross-modal transfer
   mechanism, not cohort scale.

3. Reproducibility artefact. We release manifests, training scripts,
   repeated-run summaries, mechanism controls, and a prepared
   BIMCV-COVID19+ next-cohort manifest (638 same-patient subjects,
   3,080 radiographs) as a public Code Ocean capsule
   (https://codeocean.com/capsule/6030764/tree) so that subsequent
   studies can be evaluated under a uniform evidential floor.

RESPONSES TO MAJOR REVIEWER CONCERNS

Concern (iii): The primary paired cohort is extremely small and cannot
support a strong journal-level technical claim.

Response: We have extended the primary paired cohort from 226 to 510
same-patient BIMCV CT-X-ray pairs (4.5x) using all 113 positive and
397 negative paired patients available in the BIMCV release, addressing
the cohort-scale concern directly. Under this extended cohort,
multi-slice CT teachers (mid-slice and 3-slice) now pass the
pre-specified teacher upper-bound gate with substantial effect sizes
(DeltaBA +0.045 and +0.051, 95% bootstrap CI lower bounds > +0.019),
confirming that the teacher modality carries patient-level disease
information. The cross-modal logit KD, however, fails across all four
teacher representations at this scale, with DRR-guided gated KD showing
a catastrophic specificity collapse (mean DeltaBA = -0.064, 95% CI
[-0.095, -0.034]; Table in Appendix Section "Extended 510-Patient
Same-Patient Paired BIMCV Cohort"). We interpret this as definitive
negative-result evidence at the requested cohort scale, and we have
re-framed Contribution 2 accordingly. The 226-patient calibration
near-pass (CI just touching zero) is now confirmed as a small-sample
artifact rather than an imminent positive signal.

WHY TCSVT

The problem -- leveraging a richer visual modality during training while
constraining inference to a lightweight single-modality path -- is a
general efficient visual recognition problem as much as a medical one.
The distillation machinery (logit transfer, attention transfer, feature
hints, and four published generic KD baselines) and the evaluation
protocol (same-case resampling, balance-sensitive metrics, Wilcoxon
significance testing) are directly transferable to other cross-modal
visual classification settings studied within the TCSVT community.

The falsification framing is also a direct response to the reproducibility
and benchmarking concerns raised in the broader computer vision and medical
AI literature: a transfer mechanism that cannot demonstrate a statistically
reliable balance-sensitive advantage under same-case evaluation should not
be presented as validated, regardless of how it ranks on a fixed split.
We believe this methodological contribution is useful to the TCSVT
community independently of the specific COVID imaging domain.

SCOPE AND ORIGINALITY

This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under
consideration elsewhere. The work uses only publicly released,
de-identified imaging data (Cohen et al., COVID-19 Image Data Collection)
and requires no additional ethics approval. No patient-identifiable
information is reported or released.

We have prepared a detailed supplemental appendix covering per-seed
instability tables, module ablation summaries, representative error
analysis, imbalance-sensitive controls, threshold sensitivity sweeps,
progressive complexity experiments, a category-level cross-source
non-COVID control, CT teacher representation comparisons, and the
extended 510-patient cohort results. This material is intended to make
every claim in the main text fully auditable without requiring the
reviewer to run the released code.
