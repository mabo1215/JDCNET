Cover Letter ? Revision
Submission to IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems for Video Technology (TCSVT)

Title: JDCNet: Confidence-Gated Privileged-Modality Distillation for
       Cost-Preserving X-ray Inference

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Dear Editor,

We submit a major revision of the manuscript and thank the reviewers for
their detailed and constructive feedback. The revision reorganises the
paper around a cost-preserving visual-systems question: can same-patient
CT be used as training-only privileged supervision to improve an X-ray
classifier while leaving the deployed inference graph unchanged? The
revised manuscript presents JDCNet as a confidence-gated auxiliary
supervision mechanism, reports its bounded positive evidence on the
510-patient same-patient paired BIMCV cohort, and retains the broader
mechanism audit as evidence of which cross-modal transfer channels do
not pass the same gate.

WHAT THE PAPER ADDRESSES

Chest X-ray and CT can be co-acquired for the same patient, whereas
many deployed models receive only one modality at inference time. We
therefore study training-only CT supervision for an X-ray-only deployed
classifier. This setting is difficult because teacher and student images
come from different modalities, soft-logit distillation can transfer
misleading information across the modality gap, and small paired cohorts
can make split-dependent gains look convincing. The revised manuscript
uses patient-level cross-validation, seed-paired comparisons, and a
pre-specified transfer gate to keep the claim bounded by the available
paired evidence.

WHAT THE PAPER CONTRIBUTES

1. JDCNet: confidence-gated privileged-modality distillation for
   cost-preserving X-ray inference. JDCNet uses the CT teacher's argmax
   target (hard variant) or temperature-scaled distribution (soft-KL
   variant) as an auxiliary supervisory signal only on samples where the
   CT teacher confidence exceeds a gate threshold, while the ordinary
   weighted cross-entropy on the ground-truth label remains always on.
   On 510 same-patient paired BIMCV patients evaluated under 5-fold
   patient-level cross-validation and seeds 42--44, two configurations
   pass the pre-specified transfer gate (mean DeltaBA >= +0.03 and 95%
   bootstrap CI lower bound > 0 versus the matched supervised X-ray
   baseline):

       - 3-slice CT teacher, soft-KL target, tau=0.70, lambda=1.0:
         DeltaBA = +0.0345, 95% CI [+0.0112, +0.0571], 10/15 positive
         fold-seed cells.
       - Mid-slice CT teacher, hard target, tau=0.80, lambda=1.5:
         DeltaBA = +0.0329, 95% CI [+0.0074, +0.0584], 10/15 positive
         fold-seed cells.

   The deployed student is structurally identical to the supervised
   X-ray ResNet-18 baseline (11.178 M parameters and 1.819 GMACs in the
   local sanity check), so the gain is obtained without adding CT input,
   teacher branches, or extra inference-time modules.

2. A matched mechanism audit under the same gate. The revised manuscript
   separates the JDCNet result from comparators that use the same
   student backbone and data splits. Confidence-gated logit distillation
   across four CT teacher representations, CLIP-style contrastive
   alignment, attention transfer, feature-hint distillation, BiomedCLIP
   fine-tuning, and the historical module-augmented logit-KD scaffold do
   not pass the same transfer gate. This comparison isolates the
   confidence-gated auxiliary target as the transfer channel supported by
   the current data, rather than attributing the result to a larger
   backbone, a richer teacher representation, or extra deployment-time
   modules.

3. Reproducibility and bounded interpretation. The original transfer
   gate and Method-2 candidate route are archived at commit
   9e99413e9cd1157592aaad5a2152190afa126043; the extension-sweep
   decision is archived at commit 0e1e626aa69cc96513ec1306aea6f743ad3d4e98;
   and the validated JDCNet result snapshot is archived at commit
   096ed2948544d36194cd06af51462fb218930db4. We release manifests,
   training scripts, per-cell decision reports, and the Code Ocean
   capsule (https://codeocean.com/capsule/6030764/tree) so that the gate
   and mechanism comparisons can be audited. The manuscript explicitly
   treats the effect size as bounded to this public paired cohort and
   does not make a clinical-readiness claim.

RESPONSES TO MAJOR REVIEWER CONCERNS

Concern (iii): The primary paired cohort is extremely small and cannot
support a strong journal-level technical claim.

Response: We extended the paired-cohort analysis from the earlier small
balanced subset to all 510 same-patient BIMCV paired patients used in the
headline experiment (113 positive and 397 negative). The revised paper
uses this 510-patient cohort as the sole headline evidence source and
moves smaller-cohort results to the appendix as historical feasibility
material. Under the same patient-level 5-fold protocol, CT teachers pass
the upper-bound feasibility gate (DeltaBA = +0.045 and +0.051 with
positive CI lower bounds), and JDCNet passes the transfer gate in two of
sixteen pre-specified configurations. The earlier 226-patient near-pass
for gated logit KD is now reported as small-cohort context rather than as
headline evidence.

Concern: The previous submission reported a negative result and was
difficult to position as a positive scientific contribution.

Response: The revision foregrounds the gate-passing JDCNet mechanism and
uses the negative comparator outcomes to support mechanism isolation. The
paper now asks which training-only CT-to-X-ray transfer channel survives
a matched, patient-level evidence gate. JDCNet is the only tested channel
that does so in the current cohort, while logit-KD, contrastive,
attention-transfer, feature-hint, foundation-model, and
module-augmented alternatives do not.

Concern: Risk of post-hoc cell selection within the JDCNet sweep matrix.

Response: The transfer gate and extension decisions were committed before
the supporting extension sweeps were executed, as documented by the
commit hashes above and cited in the Statistical Protocol subsection. The
main text reports all 16 JDCNet configurations in the appendix, not only
the two passing cells. Fifteen of the sixteen configurations have a
positive mean DeltaBA, which supports a bounded direction-of-effect
interpretation rather than a single post-hoc winner.

WHY TCSVT

The contribution is relevant to TCSVT's scope on efficient visual
systems: JDCNet exploits a richer auxiliary modality during training but
preserves an X-ray-only ResNet-18 inference graph. The mechanism
comparison (soft-logit, confidence-gated auxiliary targets, contrastive
alignment, attention transfer, feature hints, and foundation-model
students) is also relevant to broader cross-modal visual classification
settings where one modality may be available during training but not at
inference.

The pre-specified gate and public decision records address reproducibility
concerns in visual learning and medical imaging. The manuscript presents
a positive transfer result, but it also states its limits: one public
paired cohort, binary COVID-19/non-COVID classification, modest effect
size, and a need for independent paired-cohort replication before any
clinical or production deployment claim.

SCOPE AND ORIGINALITY

This manuscript has not been published previously and is not under
consideration elsewhere. The work uses publicly released, de-identified
imaging data and does not redistribute patient pixels. No
patient-identifiable information is reported or released. We have
prepared a supplemental appendix covering the full 16-cell JDCNet sweep,
the comparator-mechanism audit, CT teacher upper-bound checks, historical
resampling references, MIDRC stress tests, calibration scans, and module
ablations so that the main-text claims can be audited from the released
artifacts.

We appreciate your time and look forward to the review.
